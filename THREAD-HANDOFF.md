## Tree-canopy assignment context

The assignment involved:
- reading Justin Murphy’s article on Rochester’s tree-canopy inequities,
- identifying no-shade and high-shade neighborhoods,
- matching schools to those neighborhoods,
- and using READY School Finder to compare school demographics and ratings.

Primary sources used in this thread:
- `https://centerforhealthjournalism.org/our-work/reporting/rochesters-trees-are-celebrated-not-everyone-gets-have-their-time-shade`

## Pattern statement saved from this thread

The working discussion takeaway from this thread was:

- no-shade schools in northeast / low-canopy neighborhoods were overwhelmingly non-White and often aligned with weaker published school ratings,
- high-shade comparison neighborhoods more often aligned with stronger ratings and higher white-teacher percentages,
- and the user wanted this framed as a pattern of overlapping environmental inequity, racialized investment, and school opportunity, rather than as a simplistic one-cause argument.
